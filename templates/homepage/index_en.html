Products-Dr. Nova

  • Medical Language Processing
  • Genetic and Rare diseases
  • Tumor Gene Expert
  • Intelligent interpretation of genetic testing results based on NLP
  • Automated and intelligent processing including the description of phenotypic abnormalities with standardized vocabulary in Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO). No medical knowledge is required for users
  • Providing information of related phenotypes and genetic variants (such as similar variants, alterations to protein functions, pathogenicity) by real-time updating of over 15 clinical databases and automated query against over 20 million biological and medical papers
  • Standardized interpretation and report based on American College of Medical Genetics (ACMG) guidelines.
  • Create our own quantitative ranking system for variation pathogenicity。
  • Clinical practice is often complicated and ambiguous. Doctors could have different description and classification of the disease status, leading to distinct interpretation of genomic testing results
  • At present, it is difficult for medical professionals, scientific researchers, gene testing providers and patients to take advantage of standardized vocabulary such as Chinese Human Phenotype Ontology Consortium CHPO
  • Medical Language Processing Expert translates non-standardized symptom descriptions with standardized vocabularies based on natural language processing. Thus medical professional, scientific researchers and patients can still communicate accurately with their common languages
  • Cancer panels use next generation sequencing (NGS) technology to target specific genes or mutations that have established relevancy to a particular cancer phenotype. NGS facilitates sequencing of large genomic regions, high numbers of genes, and/or high numbers of samples in a single, efficient and cost-effective assay. In addition, NGS provides significantly higher sensitivity than traditional techniques, which permits the discovery of rare somatic mutations, many of which have been identified as important cancer drivers.
  • Cancer panels provide comprehensive genomic analysis for cancer research, biomarker discovery, and patient stratification for clinical discovery. Designed and developed with the most recent research data available, these assays provide thorough assessments on cancer genes of interest.
